# ImtehanHub vs the alternatives

**ImtehanHub is one of several ways a Pakistani student can prepare for board exams.** This page compares it honestly against the four most common alternatives: traditional tuition academies, past-paper booklets, other paid MCQ apps, and free YouTube / PDF channels. Same student, same exam — different trade-offs.

> **TL;DR** — Tuition academy: best for accountability, worst for cost. Past-paper booklets: cheap and trusted, but flat and non-interactive. Other paid apps: comparable feature surface, often English-only, sometimes thin on content. Free YouTube: zero structure. ImtehanHub: bilingual, structured, free tier permanent, paid tier PKR 299/mo — designed to be the structured tool that complements (not replaces) school and tuition.

## Detailed comparisons

### vs Tuition academy

**When tuition academies win.** Accountability and peer pressure are real. A student who shows up four times a week to an academy will study more than one who downloads an app. Some academies have genuinely strong teachers whose explanations beat any text-based revision.

**When ImtehanHub wins.** Cost — PKR 299/mo vs PKR 3,000+ is a 10x difference. Schedule — students study when they have time, not when the academy is open. Coverage — academies focus on the local board, ImtehanHub supports all 19 Pakistani boards including Federal (FBISE), Sindh, KPK, Balochistan, AJK. Bilingual — academies in Punjab tend toward English-heavy delivery; ImtehanHub renders fully in Urdu for students who think in Urdu.

**Best together.** Many students use both — academy for explanation and accountability, ImtehanHub for practice volume and self-quizzing. The combination is stronger than either alone.

### vs Past-paper booklets

**When past-paper booklets win.** They are physical — no screen, no distraction. They are cheap (PKR 50-300 for a chapter, sometimes less). They are trusted by parents and teachers, often more than apps. Some students simply learn better on paper.

**When ImtehanHub wins.** Adaptive — wrong answers can immediately surface in review mode, with the correct answer and a textbook reference. Tracked — streaks, accuracy, weakest-chapter analysis tell the student where to focus. Bilingual at the tap of a button. Sharing — bookmarks, share-result cards, leaderboards bring the social layer past-paper booklets cannot match. Mobile — phones are where students already spend time.

**Best together.** Past papers for full-test simulation under exam conditions (no app, no distraction, timed). ImtehanHub for daily chapter-by-chapter revision and weak-spot drilling.

### vs other paid MCQ apps

There are several Pakistani MCQ apps in the same category. We won't name them — the comparison is structural, not competitive.

**When they win.** Some have larger user bases (so leaderboards feel busier), longer track records (so parents trust them more), and offer in-app tutor chat or live class layers that ImtehanHub deliberately does not.

**When ImtehanHub wins.** Bilingual model — most competitors are English-only or token-Urdu (a few labels translated, questions still English). Free tier — most competitors paywall after a small trial; ImtehanHub Free is permanent (3 tests/day). Pricing — PKR 299/mo Pro is at or below the market floor. Privacy — explicit data inventory, self-service delete, CNIC stays in your own Drive (see [Privacy overview](/docs/privacy/overview)). Open documentation — every feature is documented here, on a public docs site, including the failure modes. No competitor publishes this level of operational detail.

**Best together.** If a competitor has a feature ImtehanHub lacks (live tutor chat, specific city-board past papers), use both. ImtehanHub's free tier is permanent — it costs nothing to keep as a second source of revision.

### vs free YouTube / PDF channels

**When YouTube wins.** Explanation. A great chemistry teacher on YouTube can teach a topic in 12 minutes that takes 3 hours of reading. Pakistan has world-class educators on YouTube — Sabaq Foundation, Kips, individual teachers — and many lectures are genuinely excellent.

**When ImtehanHub wins.** Practice volume. YouTube teaches; ImtehanHub tests. After watching a lecture, students need to verify they understood — that's MCQs, short answers, long answers with textbook references. YouTube has no streak, no leaderboard, no per-topic weakness report, no bilingual toggle, no offline mode.

**Best together.** Watch the YouTube lecture for the concept. Take 5 ImtehanHub questions on that chapter for verification. Bookmark the ones you got wrong. Re-test the bookmarks the next day. This loop is hard to beat.

## What ImtehanHub deliberately does NOT do

Honest framing matters. ImtehanHub is not:

- **Not a tutor.** No live chat, no AI tutor that explains every wrong answer (AI explanations are available in Review Mode but they're a BYOK supplement, not a flagship feature). Buy a tutor if you need a tutor.
- **Not a full LMS / school management system.** No attendance, no fees, no parent-teacher communication, no homework assignments. Use a school's existing tools for that.
- **Not a video-lecture platform.** No filmed lessons, no live classes. We point students at YouTube channels and textbooks; we don't host the lectures.
- **Not the largest question bank in Pakistan.** Several competitors claim larger numbers. We focus on textbook-keyed quality over volume.
- **Not yet on iOS.** Android only, today. iOS is feature-ready but Apple Developer Account approval is pending.

The five things above are deliberate scope choices, not gaps to fix.

## Common questions

### Is ImtehanHub the cheapest option?

Past-paper booklets are cheaper on a per-month basis (one PKR 100 booklet covers one subject for ~1 month). ImtehanHub Free is free forever but limited to 3 tests/day. For unlimited tests, ImtehanHub Pro at PKR 299/mo is among the lowest-priced paid apps in the Pakistani market.

### Will ImtehanHub work without internet?

The Android app caches chapters and lets you take tests offline. Submissions queue locally and replay when you reconnect. The web version has partial offline support via service workers. See [Offline mode](/docs/mobile/offline).

### Can ImtehanHub replace tuition academies?

For accountability and in-person help, no — academies have an irreducible value. For practice volume, self-quizzing, and structured revision, yes. Most students benefit from using both.

### Is ImtehanHub better than past-paper booklets?

For interactive practice — yes. For paper-only exam simulation — no. The two serve different study phases. Buy the booklet, install the app, use each for what it's best at.
